Governance

Organizations in this Sector
Download fact sheet  Download DATA  
 JSON  CSV  

A large number of organizations worldwide use open data in this sector to improve governance. They are primarily nonprofits that use open data to monitor budgets, spending and elections, evaluate performance, and conduct issue-based advocacy. The most used types open data include budget, spending and election data, legal and demographic and social.

From Nigeria to Ukraine, organizations use data on budgets and spending to expose and prevent mismanagement and corruption. An increasing number are monitoring government contracting and procurement as well. Other good-governance organizations use open demographic and voting information to improve voter registration and representation, as well as to provide information on polling and candidates.

Open data is also a critical tool for issue-based advocacy and citizen engagement. For example, organizations use spending and performance information on health or education to reveal and deter corruption that can keep institutions from getting the funds they’re entitled to.

Use Cases

De Olho nas Metas

Platform for monitoring the progress of municipal projects in São Paulo, Brazil
  http://deolhonasmetas.org.br

Country: Brazil

Impact: Helps civil society monitor municipal projects in their community

Data Used: Education data from the Brazilian Ministry of Education and Instituto Brasileiro de Geografia e Estatística, government operations data from the municipality of São Paulo

Read More  

According to Brazilian law, in every mandate the mayor of a municipality is obliged to submit a plan describing the goals that will be implemented during his administration. These goals cover various topics ranging from environment to education, or any other areas of intervention in the city. In 2013, a new legislation enabled the creation of Participatory Councils, civil society groups that , among other things, monitor the execution of the municipal budget, public services performance indicators, and the implementation of the proposed plan within their geographical area.

Relying on data pulled from the municipality of São Paulo website, the platform “De Olho nas Metas” was created to facilitate monitoring of the progress of municipal projects. The tool also aims to  improve communication between citizens, Participatory Councils, and public officials. For a more complete and transparent view of projects, the platform also incorporates data from the Treasury Court of São Paulo and displays information on the companies that were contracted for the execution of each project.

“De olho nas metas” has also produced reports providing recommendations for the improvement of the Brazilian education system supported by the analysis of open education data (e.g. literacy rates, education budgeting) from the Ministry of Education, and the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE).

Centre for Governance & Public Accountability

Civil society organization working for promotion of public accountability and good governance in Pakistan
  http://www.c-gpa.org/

Country: Pakistan

Impact: Promotion of citizens’ voices on key governance issues and strengthening state response to citizens’ grievances

Data Used: District level education budget of various districts in Pakistan; Municipal budget data of Peshawar District; Education, health, police, legal data from Khyber Pakhtunkhwa District

READ MORE  

The Centre for Governance and Public Accountability (CGPA) was established in 2011 to improve the levels of trust between state and society in Pakistan. CGPA’s work can be grouped into 6 main areas: open governance, rule of law, democratic governance, decentralization and devolution, promotion of pluralism, and social accountability. Since the enactment of the Freedom of Information (FOI) legislation in 2013, the CGPA has focused on the implementation of this law and published extensive research on the response by government agencies in different districts in Pakistan to information requests.

As a promoter of open governance and open data, the CGPA has produced several studies on education budgeting, municipal budgeting, and others which have contributed with analyses on areas such as public education governance and service delivery, the right to drinking safe  water and sanitation, and public accountability at the local level. The CGPA is also dedicated to making public data, such as data from the School Census, available in machine-readable format for everyone to access and use. To do so, it has plans to build its own data portal to make more data available to the citizens of Pakistan.
