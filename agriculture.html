Agriculture

Organizations in this Sector
Download fact sheet  Download DATA  
 JSON  CSV  

Organizations in this sector use open data to provide solutions that better inform farmers’ decisions on managing their farms and increase their crop yields. They primarily use agriculture, environment, and geospatial data.

For example, organizations in several countries use this data to provide farmers with timely information on weather changes, pests, and diseases via targeted mobile alerts. Others use agricultural pricing data to help farmers price their products and determine the optimal time to sell them.

In a related approach, open research and scientific data in this field is being used to support sustainable farming globally.  This includes uses in research on ways to improve the provision of staple foods, optimal forages for livestock, and other work on agro-biodiversity, sustainable green living, and urban farming.

Use Cases

Farmlogs

Supporting farmers through technology to enhance crop production and reduce waste
  https://farmlogs.com/

Country: United States

Impact: Farmers benefit from tools to better measure crop management and environmental factors

Data Used: US National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration’s (NOAA) National Climatic Data Center  - rainfall, US Department of Agriculture's Farm Service Agency - farm boundaries, Department of Agriculture - soil health

Read More  

FarmLogs provides farmers with a free software and mobile application to map and analyze crop yields and environmental conditions for crop growth through government data. Their Farmlogs free Standard includes several features: Field Mapping, Scouting and Notes, Activity Tracking, Rainfall Tracking, GDD Accumulation, Soil Composition Maps, Growth Stage Analysis, Yield Maps, Input Planning. They also empower farmers to coordinate with the government to request data for their lands. On their website, FarmLogs details instructions on how to contact the US Department of Agriculture's Farm Service Agency for field boundary data. Upon receipt, FarmLogs interprets the data and creates field boundaries on mapping systems for farmers to demarcate their lands.

Utilizing publicly accessible data remains essential to building its database of information. FarmLogs also tracks rainfall activity with data from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) and the Department of Agriculture datasets to analyze soil health. Incorporating real-time data from the government easily connect farmers to focus on crop production and avoid unnecessary waste. This free feature also monitors GDD accumulation, or heat accumulation, over a plot of land as well as color coded yield maps.

RainbowAgri

A mobile internet solution that connects farmers and help them communicate, collaborate and commerce.
  http://www.rainbowagri.com/

Country: India

Impact: Help  farmers in making informed decisions throughout the life cycle of the crop.

Data Used: Market prices, Environmental data (e.g. dam water levels), and weather data

READ MORE  

India has 87 million rural mobile Internet users, making it the world’s second largest smartphone market, and a prediction of 6% growth for the agricultural sector. Still, manifold challenges lie ahead for the continuous growth of this sector. The current availability of data, in particular of open data, has an important role to play in overcoming some of these challenges. RainbowAgri is a dashboard for farmers to gather news related to agriculture, prices of commodities, authorized pesticide and seed vendors in the locality, and dam water levels.

RainbowAgri is a mobile internet solution that aims to broadly contribute to digitize agriculture, and in this way, empower farmers to increase their productivity and income. Many of the features, such as live market prices from various markets in India, the daily dam water level, and location based weather updates, rely on the use of open data. RainbowAgri fetches most of the data from India’s national open data portal, as well as from other government of India websites as means to enrich the agriculture ecosystem with more information, and help farmers making informed decisions throughout the life cycle of the crop.
