Consumer

Organizations in this Sector
Download fact sheet  Download DATA  
 JSON  CSV  

Open data is used by numerous organizations this sector to inform their strategy, optimize their operations, and better target customers. They use many types of data, including geospatial, health, demographic and social, environment, economic and transportation.

Organizations in this sector primarily use open data primarily for organizational optimization. For instance, they use data on economic statistics, especially at the local level, to develop insights into consumer trends and better target specific markets. Trade data and commodity prices are also used to inform the core operations of an organization: For instance, a number of organizations rely on trade data to determine pricing of goods and services. Similarly, demographic data and company registers are used to inform organizations on where to best locate facilities and stores. They use this type of data to evaluate competition before a product launch and identify potential suppliers and partner companies.

A number of organizations are also integrating demographic and social data such as census studies for internal market intelligence, to get deeper insights into their current and potential customers and to tailor marketing to their target segments. In another vein, supermarkets and retailers use weather and demographic data to predict sales of different items, manage their inventory, and keep their supply chains functioning.

Use Cases

Prosper Daily

A personal finance analytics company utilizing crowdsourcing and big data analytics for consumer financial protection and savings
  https://www.prosper.com/daily

Country: United States

Impact: Identifies fraudulent merchants using consumer complaint data

Data Used: Consumer Complaint Database from U.S. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)

Read More  

Prosper Daily, formerly known as BillGuard, uses big data analytics for consumer financial protection and savings. Combining data from banks, online commentary, and government databases, Prosper Daily monitors consumer complaints concerning payment cards and bank accounts. With a base of over a million users, Prosper Daily then identifies trends of fraudulent financial activity or wrongful charges. The goal is to narrow down the sources of fraudulent activity, whether it be a merchant or individual. Specific complaint trends considered include issues of overcharges, fraud, deceptive advertising or billing practices.

The U.S. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau’s (CFPB) open Consumer Complaint Database aggregates consumer responses and directs them to companies for potential resolution. With CFPB open data services, Prosper Daily uses publicly available datasets to resolve credit card transactions issues. These datasets combined with their algorithm help users identify sources of fraudulent activity and directly contact merchants through their mobile application. In 2014, Prosper Daily (then BillGuard), claims to have flagged $60 million in suspect charges.

GoodGuide

A platform which provides users with health, environmental, and social performance information of consumer products and companies.
  http://www.goodguide.com/

Country: United States

Impact: Increase consumer knowledge of purchased products and manufacturer accountability

Data Used: Toxic Release Inventory from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, as well as dozens of other datasets

READ MORE  

In an ever-expanding market of consumer products and goods whose origins are often completely unknown to the buyer, information on where and under what conditions was a product made is hard to come by. GoodGuide works to provide consumers with this kind of information through its searchable consumer product database, ranking products such as candy, coffee and tea, food, and household chemicals, by their health, environment, and social performances.

GoodGuide draws its information from a wide variety of sources, from scientific institutions to NGOs to open government data sources. Its algorithm takes these datasets and ranks products correspondingly. For example, if a user were to look up Teas, they would find Frontier’s and Twinings’ Green Teas, as well as 4,831 other tea products, all ranked with an aggregate GoodGuide score as well as company, environment, and social scores. These scores are associated with indicators such as the amount of recycled content in their packaging for their environmental score, or whether a product is certified fair trade for its social score.

GoodGuide improves the ability for consumers to not only learn what they are buying in terms of work conditions and company environmental and social efforts, but also to gain a level of collective purchasing power based on increased information symmetry.
